Information on ROT13

ROT13 stands for rotation-13. This menas that it rotates the letters around the alphabet by 13 positions (half of the alphabet) An example is that "A" maps to "N" because N is the 13th letter after "A" in the alphabet. Interestingly as the English alphabet has only 26 letters, this means that a ROT13 encoder is at the same time a decoder - it just needs to rotate one more time to get the other half of the alphabet. The ROT13 cipher is used in various places such in public forums when sharing spoilers to for example a TV show.
